Dental Surgery Programme Specification

INSTITUTION: University of Manchester Dental School
PROGRAMME: Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS)
DURATION: 5 years full-time

CURRICULUM OVERVIEW:
The BDS programme is designed to meet all GDC Preparing for Practice learning outcomes through an integrated spiral curriculum. Students develop clinical skills progressively from year 1 through to graduation.

LEARNING OUTCOMES:
- Comprehensive patient care and clinical competence
- Professionalism and ethical practice
- Communication and teamwork skills
- Dental knowledge and scientific understanding

ASSESSMENT STRATEGY:
Multiple assessment methods including written examinations, OSCEs, clinical assessments, and workplace-based assessments. Robust standard setting and moderation processes ensure fairness and reliability.

CLINICAL TRAINING:
Structured clinical experience with progressive patient care responsibilities. Comprehensive supervision framework with defined supervision levels. Patient safety protocols aligned with national standards.

STAFFING:
Qualified academic staff with appropriate clinical and teaching experience. Staff development programmes support continuous professional development.

QUALITY ASSURANCE:
Comprehensive quality framework with annual monitoring, external examination, and stakeholder feedback systems.
